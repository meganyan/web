    • What is Totalitarianism?

    Totalitarianism is a country where the government has total control over public and private life. The government monitored the press, published books, education, and business. The government uses propaganda, education, and the press to glorify the government or the political leader. The government uses fear to control the people and denied their basic rights. Any opposition that demonstrates any opposition towards government policies or beliefs would be rooted out. Read how Joseph Stalin build his totalitarian state.

    Russia's Agricultural Revolution

    Historically before any industrial revolution, there needs to be an agricultural revolution. Most peasants in Russia were cultivating the land as in medieval times. Peasants were "collectivized" to create large scale farms using modern machinery and techniques. The government took the crops from the peasants to feed the workers who are working in the cities. Of course, the peasants revolted but Stalin deported them and took their lands.
